Makhana Farming in Bihar: Tradition, Operations, and Export Readiness

Bihar remains the most important geography for makhana cultivation in India. The sector combines traditional know-how with increasingly structured processing systems to support domestic demand and international buyers.

Core farming regions

Darbhanga, Madhubani, Purnea, Katihar, Saharsa, and Supaul are among the most active districts in this ecosystem.

Cultivation to popping cycle

  • Wetland and pond preparation based on seasonal cycles.
  • Seed sowing and growth under controlled water conditions.
  • Manual collection of mature fruits from ponds.
  • Drying, roasting, and popping to create edible kernels.

Where quality is made or lost

Moisture handling, sorting discipline, and roasting consistency directly influence final quality grade. High-performance suppliers treat post-harvest controls as non-negotiable.

Export implication for buyers

As systems mature, buyers gain better consistency in size, cleanliness, and repeatability. That makes long-cycle wholesale planning more reliable for brands and distributors.
