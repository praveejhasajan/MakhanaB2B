Makhana Farming in Bihar: Tradition, Innovation and Global Reach

By Makhanabazar Research Team | September 2025

Overview

Makhana (fox nut or Euryale ferox) is mainly cultivated in Bihar's wetlands. Bihar contributes the majority share of India's makhana production and is central to its domestic and export growth.

Major Cultivation Regions

Darbhanga, Madhubani, Purnea, Katihar, Saharsa, and Supaul are among the primary districts known for large-scale makhana cultivation.

Traditional Farming Techniques

  • Pond Preparation: Farmers prepare ponds and manage water levels for healthy seed germination.
  • Seed Sowing: Seeds are sown in shallow water, and plants spread over the pond surface.
  • Harvesting: Mature fruits are collected manually, often by skilled workers.
  • Popping: Seeds are dried, roasted, and popped to produce edible kernels.

Modern Innovations

  • Improved popping and drying methods for better consistency.
  • Research-backed training for quality and yield optimization.
  • GI recognition strengthening market position and branding.

Challenges and Opportunities

  • Fragmented landholdings and climate dependency remain key constraints.
  • Better aggregation, logistics, and market linkages can significantly improve farmer income.

Global Demand and Export Potential

Growing demand in the USA, UK, UAE, Australia, and other markets is creating sustained opportunity for export-grade raw and value-added makhana products from Bihar.
