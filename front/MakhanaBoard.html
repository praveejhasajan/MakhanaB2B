Introduction

Makhana, also known as fox nuts or lotus seeds, has gained popularity as a superfood. Not only is it rich in essential nutrients, but it also offers a wide range of health benefits, making it a fantastic addition to a balanced diet.

What is Makhana Board?

In the Union Budget of 2025-26, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman announced the establishment of a Makhana Board in Bihar. The Makhana Board is a government initiative aimed at promoting the cultivation, processing, and marketing of makhana (fox nuts), primarily in Bihar, where a significant portion of India's makhana is produced.

Who Can Benefit from the Makhana Board?

Farmers, small business owners, food processors, and exporters involved in the makhana industry can benefit from the board's programs and support.

Where is Makhana Commonly Cultivated?

Makhana (fox nuts) and Bihar share a deep connection, as Bihar is the largest producer of makhana in India, contributing to over 90% of the country's production.

  • Ideal Climate & Geography: The wetlands and ponds in Bihar, especially in districts like Darbhanga, Madhubani, Purnea, Katihar, and Saharsa, provide the perfect conditions for makhana cultivation.
  • Traditional Cultivation: Makhana farming has been a part of Bihar's culture for centuries, with traditional methods of collection, drying, and roasting.
  • High Quality: Bihar’s makhana is known for its large size, crunchiness, and superior taste, making it highly demanded in both domestic and international markets.

Key Elements of Makhana Board Initiatives

  • Farmer Support: The board will provide training to farmers, ensuring they benefit from relevant government schemes and adopt best practices in makhana cultivation.
  • Processing and Value Addition: By improving processing techniques and promoting value addition, the board aims to increase the profitability of makhana products.
  • Market Access: Efforts will be made to enhance market access, regulate pricing, and promote exports, thereby expanding the reach of makhana both domestically and internationally.

Conclusion

Makhana is a versatile and nutritious food that can benefit people of all ages. With the establishment of the Makhana Board, farmers and businesses will receive enhanced support, ensuring better production, processing, and market access. Whether you are looking to improve your heart health, control blood sugar levels, or expand your makhana business, this initiative is a significant step forward. Try incorporating makhana into your diet and experience the benefits firsthand.
